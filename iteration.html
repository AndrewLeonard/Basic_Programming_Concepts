Iteration (looping)

Applying a function repeatedly.

Iteration is a bit like a car going round a roundabout.

If the driver of the car doesn't take an exit from the roundabout, the car will just keep going round and round the roundabout.

However if the driver of the car is told that when you have gone round the roundabout 10 times you must exit the roundabout, then the "loop" is broken.

 

Two types of iteration.

1 - A never ending loop, goes on forever.

2 - A loop that goes round and round until something happens within the loop to make it exit the loop.

 

We normally only use the 2nd type of loop, as the first loop slows the program and computer down, it is a bit like a hamster in a running wheel, it goes round and round and round and not going anywhere.

Iteration is used for a variety of reasons in coding.
